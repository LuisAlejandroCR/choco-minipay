How it works

Three steps. One wallet approval.

01

Command

Text or speak your intent — amount, recipient, schedule. Agent Choco parses it and prepares the on-chain action.

"Send $50 to Mama on the 5th each month"
02

Confirm

Review the route, fee, and schedule in your wallet. Sign once. Your USDC stays in your wallet until the run date.

Wallet signs → ChocoGateway locks
03

Receipt

Choco executes on schedule. KESm arrives at the recipient. An on-chain receipt is written to ChocoLedger — permanent, verifiable, yours.

Why Choco

Not P2P. Not custodial. Not fragile.

Most remittance apps hold your money or rely on P2P liquidity that disappears at the worst moment. Choco is different by design.

🔐

Your keys, always

Choco is non-custodial. ChocoGateway holds a single locked run — not your whole balance. You can reclaim at any time. No one else holds your funds.

⛓️

On-chain liquidity, not P2P

KESm is settled via Mento's on-chain exchange — not a Binance P2P order or a partner's fiat wallet. No "liquidity unavailable" errors at 6 AM.

📜

Every receipt is permanent

ChocoLedger stores every schedule, run, and settlement on Celo Mainnet. Not in your inbox — on a blockchain anyone can verify, forever.

🌍

Multi-corridor, coming fast

Kenya (KESm) is live. Nigeria, Ghana, Colombia (Bre-B), Brazil (Pix), and Mexico (SPEI) are next — backed by Bridge, Kotani Pay, and Mento.
